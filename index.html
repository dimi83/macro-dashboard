Valuation — Shiller P/E (CAPE)
Cyclically adjusted P/E ratioMonthly
Smooths earnings over 10 years to remove boom/bust cycles. Long-run average ~17. Above 30 = historically expensive. Dashed lines = average (17) and caution zone (30).
Sentiment — VIX fear gauge
CBOE Volatility Index (VIX)Daily
Expected market volatility for the next 30 days. Below 20 = calm. 20–30 = elevated stress. Above 30 = high fear. Spiked to 65 during the COVID crash, 80+ in the 2008 financial crisis.
Recession signal — yield curve
10-year minus 2-year Treasury spreadDaily
When negative (inverted), this has preceded every US recession since the 1970s. Red shading = inversion zone. The zero line is the critical threshold to watch.
Valuation — Buffett indicator
Total US stock market cap as % of GDPQuarterly
Warren Buffett's favourite big-picture valuation gauge. 100% = market equals GDP. Above 150% = historically stretched. Below 75% = historically undervalued territory.
