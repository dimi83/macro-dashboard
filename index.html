Valuation
Shiller P/E ratio (CAPE)
Monthly
Cyclically adjusted price-to-earnings. Averages earnings over 10 years to smooth cycles. Historical average is ~17. Above 30 has historically signalled expensive markets. Dashed line = long-run average.
Sentiment & volatility
VIX — CBOE Volatility Index
Daily
Measures expected market volatility over the next 30 days. Below 20 = calm. 20–30 = elevated stress. Above 30 = fear / potential crisis. Known as the "fear gauge" of Wall Street.
Recession indicator
Yield curve — 10Y minus 2Y Treasury spread
Daily
The difference between 10-year and 2-year US Treasury yields. When negative (inverted), it has preceded every US recession since the 1970s. The zero line is the critical threshold. Red shading = inversion zone.
Buffett indicator
Buffett indicator — Wilshire 5000 / US GDP
Quarterly
Total US stock market value as a percentage of GDP. Warren Buffett called this "probably the best single measure of where valuations stand at any given moment." Above 100% = market above GDP. Above 150% = historically stretched.
